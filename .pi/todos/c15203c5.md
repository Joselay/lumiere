## Why
Current trade sizes are fixed per asset with max-position caps. Fixed size ignores volatility, recent drawdown, signal confidence, and account equity. Profit maximization needs survival-first sizing.

## Scope
- Add position sizing based on equity %, volatility/ATR, max risk per trade, and max portfolio exposure.
- Add optional stop-loss, take-profit, trailing stop, and time-in-trade exit rules to strategies/backtests.
- Add drawdown-based de-risking: reduce size after losses, stop after losing streak or negative rolling expectancy.
- Make sizing and exits part of backtest and paper/live reports.

## Acceptance criteria
- A strategy can request a target exposure, but risk manager clamps it to configured risk budgets.
- Backtests include risk-of-ruin style metrics: largest loss, losing streak, drawdown duration, and exposure over time.
- Tests cover size clamping and drawdown de-risking.
