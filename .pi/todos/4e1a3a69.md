## Why
Current live orders are market orders. On 1m BTC/ETH signals, the observed/modelled edge is often only a few bps while taker fee + spread + slippage can exceed it. `OKX_ORDER_TYPE=limit` exists, but strategies do not produce limit prices or maker/post-only order management.

## Scope
- Add explicit execution policy: market, marketable limit, post-only maker, cancel/replace timeout.
- Generate safe limit prices from order book and signal urgency.
- Simulate maker fills, non-fills, queue delay, partial fills, and adverse selection in backtest/paper.
- Measure realized spread capture vs missed-trade opportunity cost.
- Add risk guard to disable maker mode if adverse selection or non-fill rate is too high.

## Ready when
We know whether maker execution improves net PnL after missed fills and can enforce the better execution policy automatically.

## Completion evidence — 2026-06-24

Implemented maker/limit execution experiments and enforcement:

- Added explicit execution policies: `market`, `marketable_limit`, `post_only_maker`, including post-only cancel/replace timeout metadata.
- Live/demo order construction now builds marketable-limit and post-only prices from current top-of-book via `OKXDemoClient.fetch_orderbook_top` and `order_request_for_execution`.
- Backtests and paper trading simulate maker fills, non-fills, queue partial fills, fill delay, realized spread capture, adverse selection, and missed-trade opportunity cost in `execution_quality` / paper ledger events.
- Added risk guard fields `max_maker_non_fill_rate` and `max_maker_adverse_selection_bps`; maker decisions are blocked when rolling maker quality is too poor.
- Added settings/env/docs/CLI flags for maker execution experiments and risk-audit enforcement for post-only maker mode.
- Added behavior coverage in `tests/test_execution_policy.py`.
